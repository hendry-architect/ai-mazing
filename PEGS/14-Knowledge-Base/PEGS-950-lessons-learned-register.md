# PEGS-950 — Lessons Learned Register

> **The four questions. Nothing more. Nothing less.**
> Every Mission, every Quarter, every Summit, every major project ends
> with exactly:
>
> **1. What worked?**
> **2. What created friction?**
> **3. What should become standard?**
> **4. What should never happen again?**
>
> By Year Five this register is one of the most valuable assets the
> Enterprise owns: institutional memory. Entries are `LL-YYYY-NNN`,
> immutable once filed; answers that should become standard graduate to
> canon by PR (Art. VI §2) — the register remembers, the canon commands.

---

## LL-2026-001 — Mission-001, Phase A (validation sprint) · filed 2026-07-20

**1. What worked?**
The entire mission planned through existing PEGS artifacts — nineteen
dossier elements, zero new machinery. The Five Gates and design
principles measurably improved the venue/dates decision (the Option C
trade-off surfaced only because the memo forced it).

**2. What created friction?**
The decision-memo template lacks the principles-engaged line (added by
hand). Every spend routes to the Founder because thresholds are unset.
Quote-gathering has no owner but the Founder — the secretary function
gap, felt on the first real task.

**3. What should become standard?**
Decision matrices (hotel/airfare pattern) for any multi-option
procurement — promote to template on second use. The mission dossier's
component-map discipline: every new effort must map its needs to
existing artifacts before creating anything.

**4. What should never happen again?**
Planning a season-constrained commitment without a market deadline on
the dashboard from day one — the Aug 15 hold deadline was added early
this time; it must never be discovered late.

## LL-2026-002 — Calendar reconciliation from a stale baseline · filed 2026-07-20

**1. What worked?**
The reconciliation itself: a projection surfaced real gaps (mis-sorted
Aug 15 row, missing discrete Jan 5 T+72h row, budget artifacts absent
from the hold and T-30 gates), and the deltas landed in canon within the
hour, cleanly versioned.

**2. What created friction?**
The proposal was drafted against v0.1.0 while canon stood at v1.0.1 —
it re-fixed what was already fixed, carried a version *downgrade*, and
silently dropped a non-Summit row (Dec 7 review) while stating "no
non-Summit rows changed." Export round-tripping also mangled frontmatter
and links. Applied verbatim, it would have regressed ratified canon.

**3. What should become standard?**
Any externally drafted change states the canon version it was built
against, and the CGO applies external drafts **as deltas, never as file
replacements** — diff against canon first, take only what canon lacks.
(Standard adopted in practice with this entry; graduates to the sync SOP
on second occurrence.)

**4. What should never happen again?**
A wholesale file overwrite of ratified canon from any projection —
Claude Project, Notion, or otherwise. Projections propose; canon is only
ever edited in place, with its history.

## Register rules

Filed within 72h of the closing event, by the event's owner, four
answers only — an entry that needs a fifth section is hiding a decision
or an action, which belong in their own registers · "should become
standard" items get a canon PR or a tracker row in the same act · this
register feeds the annual index and the constitutional review.
