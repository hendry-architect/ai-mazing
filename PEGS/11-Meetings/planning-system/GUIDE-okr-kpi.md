# Writing OKRs and KPIs that survive contact with reality

## The distinction (most planning failures start here)

- **OKRs** = the *change* we're making this quarter. Ambitious, temporary,
  graded and retired.
- **KPIs** = the *health* we guard permanently. Stable, thresholded,
  alarmed — a KPI breach outranks OKR progress every time.

## OKR rules

1. Objective = qualitative, inspiring, one line ("Make Saturday access real
   for working families").
2. Key results = measurable and dated; a stranger could grade them.
   Bad: "improve patient experience." Good: "Google rating ≥4.8 with ≥30
   new reviews by Mar 31."
3. 3–5 objectives max per level; each KR has ONE owner.
4. Grade at 0.0–1.0; target ~0.7 on stretch KRs. Sandbagging and heroics
   are both planning failures.
5. Outcome over output: "launch the page" is output; "50 bookings via the
   page" is outcome.

## KPI register (the standing table — lives with the entity dashboard)

| KPI | Definition (exact formula) | Source of truth | Floor | Target | Owner | Review |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| e.g. New-patient bookings/wk | ChARM new-patient appts kept | ChARM report | 15 | 25 | Ops lead | Weekly |

Rules: every KPI has an exact formula and a named source system — two
people computing it must get the same number. Floors trigger action
automatically (weekly meeting §6), not debate about the formula.

## Enterprise starter set (adapt per entity)

Clinic: new-patient bookings · kept-appointment rate · review rating ·
days-cash. Media/brand: qualified reach · sends/saves · email list growth.
Publishing: units + read-through. Enterprise: cash runway · firewall
incidents (target: 0) · % decisions with written record.
