# The Annual Planning Cycle (October → January 15)

> One cycle, four movements, ending in a ratified plan and budgets by
> January 15 (annual-plan template rule). The Strategic Enterprise Summit
> is the cycle's centerpiece — the plan is pressure-tested where the
> Enterprise thinks in decades.

## The four movements

| Movement | Window | What happens | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Truth** | October | Annual KPI Review opens (PROCESS-annual-kpi-review): full-year data gathered per entity; what compounded, what stalled | KPI findings pack |
| **2. Draft** | November | 2027 annual plan drafted (TEMPLATE-annual-plan): theme, 3–5 objectives, KPI commitments with floors, initiatives, the NOT-doing list; entity budgets drafted (TEMPLATE-annual-budget) | Plan v0.5 + budget drafts |
| **3. Test** | December — **the Summit** | Plan pressure-tested at the Strategic Enterprise Summit (framework doc): decade questions first, then the plan against them; advisors/family voices per summit design | Plan v0.9 + summit report |
| **4. Ratify** | By January 15 | Founder ratifies plan + budgets (recorded, D-ID); Q1 plan locked; 2027 governance calendar issued; cycle retrospective one-pager | Ratified plan · budgets · calendar |

## Cascade rules

Annual objectives → quarterly OKRs (a quarterly objective serving no
annual objective is cut or the plan is amended — never silently both) ·
KPI floors become dashboard alarms ⚙ · every initiative gets its brief
(TEMPLATE-initiative-brief) before funds move · the NOT-doing list is
read aloud at ratification — discipline is a ceremony too.

## Inputs the cycle consumes (the knowledge loop paying off)

The annual index (`records/2026/annual-index.md`) · four quarterly
reports · the decision log's year · the risk register · KPI review
findings · summit conclusions. Memory is not consulted; the record is.

## Amendment mid-year

Only by written Founder decision with a D-ID (annual-plan rule) — plans
change deliberately or not at all.
