# The Annual KPI Review (October–November, feeding the planning cycle)

> The yearly interrogation of the numbers we guard: are these still the
> right KPIs, are the floors right, and what did a year of data teach us?
> OKRs are graded quarterly; KPIs are *re-chartered* annually — this is
> where.

## The process

| Step | Window | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Full-year pull ⚙ | Oct wk 1 | Every KPI's 12-month series from its source-of-truth system (per the register's exact formulas — two people computing must match) |
| 2. Entity sessions | Oct–Nov | Per entity (Pascual Inc., Wellnex, AllMed, Holora when licensing revenue starts): 60-min session per the agenda below |
| 3. Register re-charter | Nov | Each KPI: KEEP (floors/targets reaffirmed or moved, with reasons) · RETIRE (stopped mattering — recorded why) · ADD (what the year exposed as unguarded) |
| 4. Findings pack | Nov wk 3 | One document: trends, breaches + responses, re-chartered register, three sentences per entity a family member could read |
| 5. Feed forward | Dec–Jan | Findings → Summit truth block → annual plan §3 (KPI commitments) → dashboard alarm updates ⚙ |

## Entity session agenda (60 min, Kit applies)

1. The year in trend lines (15m) — every KPI's series, no commentary yet
2. Breach review (15m) — each floor breach: what happened, what we did,
   did the response work
3. The formula audit (10m) — is each definition still exact and still
   measuring what matters?
4. Re-charter decisions (15m) — keep/retire/add with owners and floors
5. One question (5m) — "what number were we afraid to look at this
   year?" (the honest-KPI question — answers recorded)

## Rules

A KPI without a floor is a chart, not a guard · retiring a KPI requires
stating what now guards that risk (or accepting it into the register) ·
every ADD names its source system before it enters — no aspiration
metrics · findings are written for the record, not for comfort
(Integrity before advantage).
