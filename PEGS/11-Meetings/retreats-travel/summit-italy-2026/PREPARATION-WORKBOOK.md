# Summit Preparation Workbook — everything an attendee and facilitator needs

## 1. Attendee briefing (send with the T-14 packet)

You are attending the **First Annual Strategic Enterprise Summit** —
seven days that close the Enterprise's first governed year and design its
next decade. This is a working institution with cultural and family time
built in deliberately. What's asked of you: read the packet before
travel (≈3 hours), bring your honest view of your pillar, and hold one
question all week — *what must be true by 2036?* What's promised to you:
protected mornings, one full cultural day-block, New Year's Eve as
family, and decisions made in the room, not after it.

## 2. Reading packet (T-14 — in reading order, ≈3 hours)

1. The Enterprise Charter one-pager (L01) — 5 min
2. The Annual Index 2026 (the governed year, compiled) — 40 min
3. KPI Review findings pack — 30 min
4. 2027 plan v0.5 — 40 min
5. The decade-question readings (2–3 pieces the Founder selects) — 45 min
6. Your pillar's quarterly reports (skim) — 20 min

## 3. Facilitator guide (per session — the Founder or designate)

Every session: state the output on the wall first · Meeting Kit tables
captured live (a scribe is named per session) · park anything off-output
to the parking lot (revisited Day 7) · end 10 minutes early with "what
did we decide, who owns what, by when" read back aloud.

| Session | Facilitation note |
|---|---|
| Founder Vision (D1) | Story first, numbers later — the index read ALOUD sets the register for the week |
| Pillar reviews (D2) | 45 min per pillar hard-stop; truth over completeness; misses stated in the same tone as wins |
| Capital (D3) | Decisions here get memos DRAFTED IN THE ROOM — the blanks (reserve months, philanthropy %) leave this session filled |
| Innovation (D4) | Diverge before converge; the cultural afternoon is the incubator — capture the dinner ideas that night |
| Legacy (D5) | Slowest pace of the week; the facilitator's job is silence; Trust-filing rules stated at the start |
| Plan build (D6) | v0.5 on the table at 9:00; the NOT-doing list gets equal ceremony; Declaration signed before dinner Jan 1 |
| Resolutions (D7) | Commitments spoken by each person, recorded verbatim |

## 4. Packing checklist (per attendee)

Documents: passport (valid ≥6 mo) · insurance card · printed continuity
note (Founder). Tech: PHI-clean laptop/tablet · VPN configured · offline
copies of the packet · chargers + EU adapters. Attire: business-casual
sessions · one formal (NYE dinner) · walking shoes (Riviera terrain) ·
December coastal layers (8–14°C). Personal: medications · reading
packet printed if preferred · a notebook — Day 5 is better on paper.

## 5. Session worksheets (one page each, in the packet)

Each attendee completes before arrival: **Pillar sheet** (my pillar's
year in 5 lines · the number that worries me · the opportunity nobody's
discussing) · **Decade sheet** (2036 in one paragraph · what 2027 must
start · what we must stop) · **Legacy sheet** (Day 5, family only —
prompts private to the packet).

## 6. Decision log + action tracker (summit-local)

The scribe maintains one running DECISIONS table and one ACTIONS table
across all seven days (Kit format, D-/A-IDs continue the enterprise
sequence). Read back daily at close; merged into the enterprise log and
tracker in the T+72h report — the summit never creates a second system.

## 7. Post-retreat report (T+72h — assembly map)

Trip-report shell + retreat-report sections: outcomes vs. purpose ·
the seven days in one page · DECISIONS + ACTIONS tables (merged) · the
Annual Declaration (attached) · plan v0.9 (attached) · spend vs. budget ·
Summit 2027 criteria ("repeat/change") · closed-session pointer (Trust,
not content). Filed to `records/2026/Q4/` with the annual archive.

## 8. 2027 Strategic Plan + Goals (templates in canon — no new forms)

The plan builds on `planning-system/TEMPLATE-annual-plan.md` (theme,
3–5 objectives, KPI commitments, initiatives, NOT-doing, resourcing);
goals ARE the plan's §2 objectives — stated in the Annual Declaration in
plain language, one line each.
