# Intercompany Agreements — governance substance

When the holding structure forms, entities stop sharing informally.
Everything that crosses an entity line gets a written agreement at arm's
length. Counsel drafts the instruments; these are the Enterprise's required
terms.

## 1. Shared services agreement (HoldCo → OpCos)

| Term | Standard |
|---|---|
| Services | Enumerated (finance, HR, IT, marketing ops, governance/secretary) — no "etc." |
| Pricing | Cost-plus <X>% or market benchmark; method stated, reviewed annually |
| Data | Service access ≠ data ownership; PHI never crosses into non-clinical entities (firewall) |
| Term/exit | Annual renewal; 90-day exit so no OpCo is hostage to HoldCo |

## 2. Management agreement (executive services to an entity)

Scope of management authority (mirrors the Authority Matrix — the contract
never grants what the matrix doesn't) · fees · reporting · termination.

## 3. IP licensing (brands, content, methods)

| Term | Standard |
|---|---|
| Ownership | IP sits in the designated IP holder; OpCos license, never own drift-wise |
| License | Named marks/works, field-of-use, quality control per brand canon (Brand Firewall inside a contract) |
| Royalty | Stated basis, arm's-length justified |
| The Founder's name/likeness | Licensed personally, revocable, never sold outright — SOSTENGO never sells |

## 4. Cash management (with TEMPLATE-treasury-policy.md)

Sweep arrangements documented · intercompany loans papered (rate, term,
schedule) — no undocumented balances · each entity solvent standalone; no
commingling, ever (the pierce-the-veil protection is an operating habit,
not a legal opinion).

**Rule:** every intercompany arrangement is reviewed annually against
"would we sign this with a stranger?" — if not, reprice it.
