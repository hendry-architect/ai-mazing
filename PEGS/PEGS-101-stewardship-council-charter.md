## Purpose

Establish the Stewardship Council: the body that holds the Enterprise's
constitutional (Class 1) authority when the Founder no longer does. PEGS-000
references this Council (Art. V §1, Art. VIII §3); this charter defines it, so
that succession is a documented transition rather than a crisis.

## Scope

Governs constitutional authority only. The Council does not manage entities,
approve budgets, or make Class 2/3 decisions — those belong to the roles
defined in the 200-series and to entity leadership.

## Provisions

### §1. Status: dormant by design

The Council exists on paper from ratification of this charter but holds **no
authority while the Founder is active**. The Founder is the sole Class 1
authority until an activation trigger in §2 occurs. Dormancy is not weakness;
it is the succession plan already written.

### §2. Activation triggers

The Council activates upon the first of:

1. **Written delegation** — the Founder transfers Class 1 authority, in whole
   or in part, by written instrument;
2. **Incapacity** — the Founder is medically unable to exercise judgment, as
   determined under the process in the 500-series succession documents;
3. **Death of the Founder.**

Activation is recorded in the PEGS registry. It is irreversible except by the
Founder's own written revocation under trigger 1.

### §3. Composition

- **Size:** three to seven stewards.
- **Qualification:** demonstrated adherence to PEGS-000, proven competence in
  a domain the Enterprise operates in, and character consistent with Article
  III. Family members are eligible on exactly these terms — stewardship is
  earned, never inherited (PEGS-000 Art. III §7, Art. VI §6).
- **Clinical seat:** while the Enterprise operates any Class A (clinical)
  entity, at least one steward must be a licensed physician.
- **Initial roster:** named by the Founder in a sealed 500-series document,
  amendable by the Founder at any time before activation.
- **Vacancies after activation:** filled by vote of the remaining stewards
  under §5, against the qualification criteria above.

### §4. Powers when active

1. Exercise Class 1 (constitutional) decisions under PEGS-000 Art. V §1;
2. Ratify amendments to PEGS-000 under Art. VIII §3 — the Immutable Core
   (Art. VIII §2) remains beyond its reach, permanently;
3. Hold custodianship of the 000-series and appoint custodians for all other
   series;
4. Confirm or replace the chief executive of each entity — its sole
   operational power, exercised as stewardship, not management.

### §5. Decision rule

- **Constitutional acts** (PEGS-000 amendments, activation-era succession
  decisions): two-thirds supermajority of all seated stewards.
- **All other Council acts:** simple majority.
- Every act requires a written record with dissents preserved (PEGS-000
  Art. V §3). No written record, no act.

### §6. Duties before activation

Once the initial roster is named, stewards-designate join the annual
constitutional review (PEGS-000 Art. VI §7). A steward who has never read the
Constitution cannot guard it.

## Decision rights created or modified

- Creates no active decision rights today; the Founder retains all Class 1
  authority.
- Pre-commits the allocation of Class 1 authority upon a §2 trigger — this
  charter *is* that decision, made calmly and in advance.
