## 1. The organizing philosophy

The Pascual Enterprise is architected as a **layered system with separated
powers**, designed for a 100-year horizon. Its central design decision:
**no single person, entity, or failure can take down the whole** — not a
lawsuit, not a bad executive, not a market collapse, not the Founder's own
absence. The Enterprise sustains because sustaining is the mission
(PEGS-000 Art. I), applied to itself.

Three separations do most of the work:

1. **Ownership is separated from management.** Owning entities (future:
   Ascendencia Trust → Atemporal Holdings LLC) hold and allocate; operating
   companies execute. An owner who cannot run a clinic can still protect
   it; an operator who leaves cannot take it.
2. **Governance is separated from ownership.** PEGS — the Constitution,
   councils, and canon — governs conduct regardless of who holds equity.
   A future heir may own much and govern nothing (stewardship is earned,
   PEGS-000 Art. III §7); a non-family CEO may govern operations and own
   nothing. This is why an acquisition must adopt PEGS-000 to close
   (PEGS-100 §5): governance travels with the canon, not the cap table.
3. **Counsel is separated from control.** Advisory bodies advise
   (04-Advisory-Council); the Stewardship Council is dormant until
   succession (PEGS-101 §1). Voices are many; accountable deciders are
   always singular and named (Art. VI §3).

## 2. The seven layers of governance

| Layer | What it decides | Canon |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Constitutional** | Identity, values, immutables | PEGS-000 (01-Constitution) |
| **2. Architectural** | Structure, relationships, authority topology | PEGS-150 (this series) |
| **3. Structural** | Which entities exist, entity lifecycle, succession bodies | PEGS-100, PEGS-101 (02-Governance) |
| **4. Directional** | Plans, budgets, capital allocation | Planning system (L05), capital policies (L09) |
| **5. Executive** | Who decides what, day to day | Role charters, authority matrices (L02) |
| **6. Operational** | How work is done | SOPs, policies, brand canons (L04, L12, 09/10) |
| **7. Memory** | What is recorded, learned, archived | Meeting Kit, Knowledge Base, Archives (L05/L06) |

Each layer derives legitimacy from the one above and may never contradict
it. A document is misfiled if it decides at the wrong layer — the
architecture's placement rule (§4) exists to catch this.

## 3. The two independences

**Operational independence.** Every operating company runs standalone: its
own charter (08-Companies), leadership, KPIs, bank accounts (Treasury
Policy — no commingling, ever), and compliance rows. If one company
stumbles, the others do not inherit its wounds. Shared services are
*contracts*, not entanglement (L09 intercompany guide).

**Legal independence.** Each entity is a distinct legal person with clean
boundaries: documented intercompany agreements at arm's length, separate
records via the Corporate Secretary function, its own insurance rows. The
protective wall around the family's legacy is an operating *habit*
practiced daily, not a legal opinion purchased once. (Conceptual only —
legal formation and tax design are counsel's work; PEGS-150.005 rule.)

## 4. The seven domains and the placement rule

Every future artifact belongs to exactly one domain. **Before any artifact
is created, its domain is determined; if it is proposed for the wrong
domain, work stops and the correct location is recommended first.**

| Domain | Home | Test: it belongs here if… |
|---|---|---|
| Enterprise Constitution | 01-Constitution | it defines identity or immutables |
| Enterprise Architecture | 02-Governance/PEGS-150 | it defines structure, relationships, or authority topology |
| Governance | 02/03/04, 11 | it defines bodies, roles, records, or rhythms |
| Operations | 08/09/10 | it directs daily execution |
| Legal | counsel's files (referenced, not stored) | it binds the Enterprise at law |
| Automation | 13-Automation | it makes machines do repeatable work |
| Knowledge | 14-Knowledge-Base | it explains but does not command |
