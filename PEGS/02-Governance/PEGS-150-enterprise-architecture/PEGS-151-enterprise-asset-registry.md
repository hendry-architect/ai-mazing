> **What this is.** The catalog of everything the Enterprise owns, holds,
> or stewards — present and future. An asset not on this registry is
> invisible to governance: unprotected, uninsured, unallocated, and
> un-inheritable. **What this is not:** a valuation, a balance sheet, tax
> advice, or a legal schedule — counsel and accountants derive those from
> it (Phase 6).

---

## 1. Registry principles

1. **One asset, one row, one custodian.** Every asset has exactly one
   accountable steward (PEGS-000 Art. VI §3).
2. **Assets ≠ people.** Employees, providers, patients, and relationships
   are never registry entries — human beings are ends, not property
   (Art. III §2). What the registry holds is the *goodwill and systems*
   around them, never them.
3. **PHI is custodial, never commercial.** Patient data appears here only
   as a protected custodial responsibility — it is held in trust under the
   clinical firewall, never treated as a monetizable asset (Art. VI §4.1).
4. **Every row maps to PEGS-152.** Each asset class carries a target
   holder per the Ownership Philosophy; the registry records current vs.
   target state.
5. **New asset → new row in the same quarter it arises**; disposals are
   marked, never deleted (the record rule, Art. VIII §3.4).

**Status marks** (consistent with PEGS-150.002): ✅ ratified/confirmed ·
🔶 declared, TO CONFIRM · 🔮 future/target.
