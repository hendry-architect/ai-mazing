> **What this is.** The long-term ownership doctrine of the Pascual
> Enterprise: who should hold what, for how long, and why — for companies,
> intellectual property, real estate, investments, charitable entities,
> and future trust structures. **What this is not:** legal drafting or tax
> advice. Counsel translates this doctrine into instruments (Phase 6);
> where an executed instrument differs, the instrument governs at law and
> this doctrine is amended to match or the instrument is renegotiated.

---

## 1. First principles of ownership

1. **Ownership is stewardship with a deed.** Nothing here is "owned" in
   the consuming sense — every asset is held in trust for the mission and
   the next steward (PEGS-000 Art. III §7).
2. **Control, economics, and management are three different things** —
   and the architecture deliberately separates them (PEGS-150.001 §1).
   Control concentrates upward (toward Trust); economics distribute per
   policy (L07); management delegates downward (to operators).
3. **Assets consolidate; operations separate.** Long-lived assets (IP,
   real estate, capital) migrate toward protected holders; operating risk
   stays quarantined inside operating companies. The Hands can be injured
   without the Backbone breaking.
4. **Patient capital, permanent horizon.** The Enterprise never sells
   under duress and never buys under euphoria; the Five Gates' Legacy
   test applies to every acquisition AND every disposal.
5. **Concentration is a named risk, not a strategy.** No single asset,
   payer, platform, or market may quietly become the whole (risk register
   R-05/R-07 logic applied to ownership).
6. **Everything confirmed, nothing assumed.** Target states below apply
   to 🔶 assets only after their registry rows confirm (PEGS-151).

## 2. Doctrine by asset class

> **The canonical doctrine statement (Founder's words):** *The intended
> long-term ownership doctrine of the Pascual Enterprise is that
> controlling interests shall ultimately reside within the approved
> enterprise ownership structure, as determined by the Founder and
> implemented with qualified legal and tax counsel.* Everything in this
> section details that sentence; where counsel's implementation refines
> the specifics below, the doctrine statement governs and this document
> is amended to match.

### 2.1 Companies (Class A)

- **Target chain (current intended implementation):** Ascendencia Trust
  🔮 → **Atemporal Holdings LLC (exists — WY LLC, integration under
  development)** → operating companies (Pascual Inc., Wellnex Academy,
  Los Gonsos, Holora). The Founder's direct holdings migrate up this
  chain on counsel's design under the doctrine statement above; entity-
  specific constraints (S-Corp shareholder rules for Pascual Inc., the
  ROBS structure at Los Gonsos) are counsel's domain and are flagged,
  not solved, by this architecture.
- **Control equity never leaves the chain.** Operators, partners, and
  investors may hold economic participation (profit interests, bonuses,
  venture-specific equity) but never control of the clinical root or of
  Holdings itself.
- **Acquisitions** enter AT the Holdings level (never merged into an
  existing OpCo's risk envelope by default) and adopt PEGS-000 at close
  (PEGS-100 §5).
- **Disposal doctrine:** an operating company may be sold when it fails
  the Five Gates two annual reviews running — but see the never-sell
  list (§3).

### 2.2 Intellectual property (Class B)

- **Target:** consolidate registrable IP (marks, filings, content
  libraries, PEGS™ itself) into **Holora Health LLC — the designated
  IPCo, which exists** — under Holdings, licensing back to operators at
  arm's length (L09 §3). The portfolio is under development: **no
  ownership is assumed to have legally transferred** until counsel
  executes each assignment and the registry row (PEGS-151 Class B)
  records it.
- **The personal carve-out — permanent:** the Founder's name, likeness,
  voice, the SOSTENGO seal, and the HP crest are held by the Founder
  **personally**, licensed to the Enterprise revocably, and are never
  assigned, sold, or absorbed into any structure — including the Trust.
  On succession they pass per the Founder's personal instruments with the
  same never-sell covenant. (PEGS-150.006 rows 17–18 enforce this at the
  decision layer.)
- **Book copyrights:** decided per work — works of the personal voice may
  stay personal; commercial/programmatic works consolidate. Each decision
  recorded in the registry row.
- **Nothing brand-critical is ever pledged as collateral.**

### 2.3 Real estate (Class E)

- **Target:** operating real estate held in **Los Gonsos Royal Estate
  Corp — the designated RE company, existing but pre-operational** —
  leased to operators at documented arm's-length terms; the building must
  survive the tenant's worst lawsuit. Its **ROBS structure** carries
  ownership and compliance constraints that counsel must document before
  any property enters it — architecture flags this, nothing more.
- Buy vs. lease is a capital-allocation decision (L09 hurdles), but once
  bought, operating premises join the century-asset class (§4).

### 2.4 Investments (Class F)

- **Target:** held in investment entities 🔮 under Holdings, funded solely
  through the waterfall's opportunity fund (150.005), within the capital
  policy's caps and Class 2 hurdles.
- Investments serve the Enterprise's endurance first, return second:
  liquidity ladders before venture bets; nothing levered against control
  assets — ever (L09).

### 2.5 Charitable entities

- **The Foundation owns mission, not operations.** It holds its endowment
  and grant capital for the community it serves; it never owns operating
  companies, never provides private benefit, and never becomes a parking
  place for assets the family wishes to control informally.
- Funding flows one way — from the waterfall's philanthropy line to the
  Foundation (150.005); mission returns are measured in the impact report
  (L08), not in dollars back.

### 2.6 Trust structures (future)

- **Ascendencia Trust 🔮 is the apex holder** — the Backbone: it owns
  Holdings, endures across generations, and separates *benefit* (family,
  per the wealth stewardship policy L07) from *control* (trustee +
  Stewardship Council logic, PEGS-101).
- The trust exists to make the Enterprise un-divisible by inheritance
  disputes, un-raidable by any single generation, and un-breakable by any
  single death. Its instrument must embed PEGS-000's stewardship
  principles by reference — architecture's requirement of counsel.
- Heirs receive formation before they receive benefit (L07 heir
  education); benefit is policy-governed, control is earned.

## 3. The never-sell list

Regardless of price, these are not for sale — a proposal to sell one is a
proposal to found a different enterprise (Art. VIII §2 logic applied to
assets):

1. The Founder's name, likeness, voice, and personal marks (§2.2).
2. Control of the clinical root while the family line stewards the
   Enterprise (the first Hands are also the heart's origin).
3. The PEGS canon and its records (they may be licensed as method, never
   alienated).
4. Patient trust in any form — data, lists, or access are never sold
   (PEGS-151 §1.3, C-03).
5. The Foundation's endowment, for any non-mission purpose.
