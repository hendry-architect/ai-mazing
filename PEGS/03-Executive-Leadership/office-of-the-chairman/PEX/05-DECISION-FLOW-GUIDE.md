# PEX™ 5 — The Decision Flow Guide
## "How I approve decisions"

Deciding is your core job, and PEGS makes it a five-minute act. Here is
the entire flow.

## 1. The memo comes to you

Anything that needs your ruling arrives as a **prepared decision memo**
— one page: what's being decided, the options, the recommendation, the
cost, what's irreversible about it, and how it touches the mission and
the firewalls. You never have to request the memo format or check it
against policy — that's done before it reaches you.

If someone brings you a decision *without* a memo, the answer is:
*"Bring it as a memo."* That sentence protects you more than any policy.

## 2. You read it (five minutes)

Ask yourself the four gate questions — they're already answered in the
memo, you're checking the answers ring true:

- Does this serve the mission?
- Would I sign my name to this publicly?
- Is this our standard of quality?
- Does this build something that lasts?

## 3. You rule — in a sentence

Reply in chat, in plain words. All of these are valid rulings:

> *"Approved. Confidence: High."*
> *"Approved with one condition: [condition]. Confidence: Medium."*
> *"Declined — revisit after Q1."*
> *"I need to know [X] before I rule."*

The **Confidence level** (High / Medium / Low) is the one piece of
metadata you attach. It costs you three words now; in a year, it tells
you which kinds of calls you were sure of and which you weren't — and
whether your sureness was earned.

## 4. Claude does the rest

Your ruling is stamped with a D-number (D-2026-002…), logged in the
Decision Log with the date, class, and confidence, linked to the memo,
and — for one-way doors — filed with your written words verbatim. Done.
That's the whole ceremony. Your word, recorded, is the institution.

## The 30-day rule (constitutional decisions only)

Class 1 decisions — changes to what the enterprise IS — carry a built-in
30-day reflection before they take effect. Nothing else does. Speed for
the reversible; deliberateness for the permanent.

## What to say to Claude

*"What's in my decision queue?"* · *"Prepare a decision memo for…"* ·
*"Approved — confidence high."* · *"Show me every decision I made this
quarter."* · *"What did I decide about [topic], and what did I say?"*
