# PEX™ 7 — The Quick Commands Manual
## "What do I type to Claude?"

You don't need commands, syntax, or exact wording — plain language
always works. These are simply the phrases that map cleanly to what the
system does. Say them your way; Claude will understand.

## Seeing where things stand

| You type | You get |
|---|---|
| "Show me my dashboard" | The one screen of everything awaiting you |
| "What needs my decision?" | The decision queue, oldest first |
| "What's overdue?" | Tracker rows past their dates, with owners |
| "Where does Mission-001 stand?" | Mission status from the record |
| "What did we decide about [topic]?" | The D-entry, with your exact words |
| "Show me the scorecard" | Whether the *system itself* is healthy |

## Deciding

| You type | What happens |
|---|---|
| "Prepare a decision memo for [thing]" | A one-page memo comes back for your ruling |
| "Approved. Confidence: High" | Ruling stamped, D-number assigned, logged |
| "Approved with condition: […]" | Same, with the condition on the record |
| "Declined — [reason]" | Logged; the reason is preserved |
| "I need [X] before I rule" | Memo goes back for the missing piece |

## Running the rhythm

| You type | What happens |
|---|---|
| "Prepare Monday's agenda" | ELT agenda built from open items |
| "File the minutes: [your notes]" | Minutes formatted, filed, actions tracked, follow-ups queued |
| "Action for [name]: [task] by [date]" | Tracker row created |
| "Compile the Chairman's Book" | The monthly PEGS-900 edition |
| "Run the quarter close" | The eight-step close, prepared for you |

## Missions and lessons

| You type | What happens |
|---|---|
| "Initiate Mission-00X: [purpose]" | Full dossier built from existing artifacts |
| "Close Mission-00X and file the lessons" | Close-out + four questions into the register |
| "File a lesson: [what you noticed]" | A PEGS-950 entry — friction noted, not forgotten |

## Brand work (your existing commands still work)

`/SOSTENGO` · `/PH` · `/PHSEO` — your personal-brand workflows run as
before; the firewall applies (nothing sells, nothing absorbed).

## The universal command

When you don't know what to ask: **"What needs me?"**
And when you don't know how something works: **just describe it in plain
words.** There is no wrong phrasing — the system's job is to understand
you, never the reverse.
