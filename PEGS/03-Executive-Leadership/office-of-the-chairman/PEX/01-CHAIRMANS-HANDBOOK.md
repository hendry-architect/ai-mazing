# PEX™ 1 — The Chairman's Handbook
## "What do I do?"

You lead. You decide. You sign. Everything else — the preparing, the
formatting, the filing, the remembering — is done for you. This page is
your whole job.

## Your week

| When | What you do | Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Monday morning** | Chair the ELT meeting (your team: you, Dr. Barroso Perez, Martha). The agenda arrives ready — you just run it. | 50 min |
| **First Monday of the month** | Chair the Monthly Review — the scoreboard, what's off-track, what to reallocate. | 90 min |
| **Friday 3 pm** | Read your Weekly Brief (one page, arrives ready). | 15 min |
| **Friday 4 pm** | Your private reflection — three questions, your own notebook. | 20 min |
| **Whenever** | Rule on decisions waiting for you (they come as prepared memos). | 5–10 min each |

## Your year

| When | What |
|---|---|
| Last week of each quarter | Quarter close + retreat: the honest report, then lock the next quarter's plan |
| October–November | Annual KPI review — a year of numbers, interrogated |
| **December 27 – January 2** | **The Ascendencia Summit™, Monte-Carlo** — the year closed, the next one born |
| **January 1** | Sign the five Annual Declarations |
| July | The Annual Constitutional Review — the ten-minute Stewardship Reading, then the honest conversation |

## Only you can do these

Approve one-way-door decisions (money, entities, contracts, hires at the
top) · anything touching your name, likeness, or the SOSTENGO brand ·
constitutional changes · clinical, legal, and patient-privacy matters
(the firewall — never delegated, never automated).

## You never have to

Open GitHub · format a document · remember a deadline (they come to you)
· write minutes · file anything · wonder where something is (ask Claude).

## When something feels wrong

Say it out loud, early. The system rewards surfaced problems and treats
hidden ones as the only real offense. If you're ever unsure what
something is or where it stands: just ask Claude, in plain words.
