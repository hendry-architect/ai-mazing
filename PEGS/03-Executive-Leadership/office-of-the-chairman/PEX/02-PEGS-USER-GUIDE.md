# PEX™ 2 — The PEGS User Guide
## "How PEGS works"

PEGS is your enterprise's operating system. Eight ideas explain all of it.

**1. One constitution.** Everything traces to PEGS-000 — your mission
(*No curo. Sostengo.*), your values, your rules for deciding. Nothing in
the enterprise may contradict it, ever.

**2. Three kinds of decisions.**
- **Class 1 — constitutional.** Changes what the enterprise IS. Rare.
  Yours alone, with a 30-day reflection built in.
- **Class 2 — one-way doors.** Hard to reverse: big money, new entities,
  key hires, contracts, anything legal. These come to you as a prepared
  memo; you rule on them.
- **Class 3 — reversible.** Everyday calls. Your team makes them, records
  them, and tells you. If they ask your permission for these, something's
  wrong with the system, not with them.

**3. If it isn't written, it didn't happen.** Every decision gets a
number (D-2026-001…), every meeting leaves minutes, every action has an
owner and a date. Claude does the writing; you do the deciding.

**4. Your word makes things official.** When you say "Approved" (in chat,
in writing), Claude records it, stamps it, and it becomes part of the
permanent record. That's the whole ceremony.

**5. Everything feeds forward.** Meetings → records → quarterly reports →
the annual index → next year's plan. The enterprise plans from its
memory, not from anyone's recollection.

**6. The four questions.** Every mission, quarter, and summit ends with:
What worked? What created friction? What should become standard? What
should never happen again? That register becomes your institutional
memory.

**7. The offices.** Nine functions with continuity — yours (the Office of
the Chairman), your executives', the portfolio office, the governance
office. People may change; the offices and their records don't.

**8. The firewalls.** Three lines nothing crosses: clinical/legal/patient
privacy stays with licensed humans · your personal brand never sells and
is never absorbed · the law is the floor under everything. No deadline
overrides a firewall.

That's PEGS. Everything else is detail Claude carries for you.
