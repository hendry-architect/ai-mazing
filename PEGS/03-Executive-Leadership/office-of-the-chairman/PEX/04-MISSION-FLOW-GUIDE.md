# PEX™ 4 — The Mission Flow Guide
## "How a mission runs"

A **mission** is a bounded piece of enterprise work with a start, an
end, a budget, and a number: M-001, M-002… Missions are for things too
big for a single decision but too finite to be an office — a summit, a
launch, an acquisition, a build-out.

## The life of a mission (five steps)

**1. You call it into being.** One sentence to Claude: *"Initiate
Mission-002: [name and purpose]."* Claude builds the dossier — purpose,
scope, phases, budget frame, risks, success criteria — from what
already exists. No new processes are invented; a mission runs through
the same decision log, tracker, and budget instruments as everything
else.

**2. You approve it.** The dossier comes to you as a decision memo. You
rule on it like any decision (see the Decision Flow Guide). Your
approval is the mission's birth certificate — it gets a D-number.

**3. It runs in phases.** Each phase has an exit gate — a short list of
what must be true before the next phase starts. Money above the mission's
frame, or anything one-way-door, comes back to you as a memo. Everything
else moves without you.

**4. It reports as it goes.** Mission actions live in the same tracker
you see every Friday. You never ask "how's the mission going?" into a
void — you ask Claude, and the answer comes from the record.

**5. It closes with the four questions.** Every mission ends with a
close-out: money reconciled, actions closed, and the four questions
answered — *What worked? What created friction? What should become
standard? What should never happen again?* — filed into the Lessons
Learned register. A mission that hasn't answered them isn't closed.

## The worked example: Mission-001

M-001 is the Ascendencia Summit™ — and it's also the proof-run of PEGS
itself. You initiated it in one message. The dossier mapped all of it to
existing artifacts. You approved it as D-2026-001, with a written
decision Claude filed verbatim. Phase B (execution) is now live: hold
the venue by Aug 15, Class 2 memo at T-60, the summit itself Dec 27 –
Jan 2, close-out with lessons filed by Jan 5. You never opened a
spreadsheet.

## What to say to Claude

*"Initiate Mission-00X: …"* · *"Where does Mission-001 stand?"* · *"What
does M-001 need from me this week?"* · *"Close Mission-001 and file the
lessons."*
