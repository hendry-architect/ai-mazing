## Preamble

This is not a legal document. It is the North Star.

It defines the small set of principles that do not change — the identity, values,
and decision logic that every future entity, executive, advisor, acquisition,
system, and family member of the Pascual Enterprise inherits on day one and
follows for as long as they carry the name.

Laws, structures, brands, and markets will change. This document exists so that
what the Enterprise *is* does not change with them.

Every other document in the Pascual Enterprise Governance System (PEGS) derives
its authority from this one. Nothing gets built before it. Nothing gets built
against it.

**Defined term:** "the Enterprise" means the Pascual Enterprise — the full
constellation of current and future entities, brands, ventures, holdings, and
initiatives founded by or descending from Dr. Hendry Pascual, including but not
limited to clinical practice, publishing, media, wellness advisory, education,
and technology ventures.

---

## Article I — Mission

**The Enterprise exists to sustain human beings — patients, readers, listeners,
teams, and generations — by building systems of care, knowledge, and wellbeing
that outlive any single person, including the Founder.**

We do not merely treat, publish, or advise. We sustain. Every entity in the
Enterprise, whatever its industry, is in the same business: helping people
carry what life asks of them — in their own language, with evidence, with
dignity, and for the long term.

---

## Article II — Vision

A multi-generational enterprise, rooted in medicine and radiating into
publishing, media, wellness, and technology, that is known for three things:

1. **Trust** — the most trusted bilingual voice in health and human
   sustainability for the communities it serves, starting in South Florida and
   extending wherever Spanish and English are spoken.
2. **Craft** — premium, museum-grade work in every medium; nothing leaves the
   Enterprise that we would not sign.
3. **Endurance** — institutions, systems, and a family legacy still operating
   with these same principles one hundred years from now.

The measure of the vision is not size. It is whether the Enterprise still
deserves its name after the Founder is no longer in the room.

## Article III — Core Values

Values are not decoration. Each value below is a constraint: it forbids real,
tempting behavior. Anyone acting for the Enterprise is expected to know them
and to be able to say, for any significant decision, which values it engaged.

### 1. Sostengo — We Sustain
We build for endurance, not rescue. Sustainable systems beat heroic
interventions; compounding beats quick wins.
*Forbidden:* quick fixes that create dependency, growth that burns out people,
products that spike and die.

### 2. Human Dignity First
The patient, the reader, the employee, and the family member are ends, never
means. Safety and dignity outrank revenue, speed, and reach — always.
*Forbidden:* fear-based marketing, shame-based messaging, manipulation of any
kind, any compromise of patient safety.

### 3. Integrity Before Advantage
We say what is true, distinguish evidence from speculation, and honor the
spirit of our commitments — especially when no one is watching and especially
when it costs us.
*Forbidden:* exaggerated medical certainty, inflated claims, quiet corner-cutting,
telling different audiences different truths.

### 4. Excellence as the Standard
Apple-keynote test, Hermès-page test: if the work would not hold on that
stage, elevate it before it ships. Premium is a discipline, not a price point.
*Forbidden:* "good enough" in anything that carries the name, cluttered work,
shipping to hit a date at the cost of the standard.

### 5. Systems Over Heroics
If it happened twice, it becomes a system. We document, template, automate,
and delegate so that excellence does not depend on any one person's memory,
energy, or presence.
*Forbidden:* undocumented critical knowledge, processes that only work when
the Founder touches them, heroic effort as a business model.

### 6. Dos Idiomas, Una Voz — Two Languages, One Voice
We are a bilingual, bicultural enterprise by identity, not by translation. We
meet people in their language, at native quality, with the same warmth and the
same standard in both.
*Forbidden:* Spanish as an afterthought, literal translation that kills
meaning, treating either community as the "secondary" audience.

### 7. Stewardship, Not Ownership
Every leader — including the Founder and every family member — holds their
role in trust for the next generation. We leave every entity, team, and
relationship stronger than we found it.
*Forbidden:* extracting value that the next steward must repay, roles granted
by surname rather than earned by competence and character.

## Article IV — Leadership Principles

These bind everyone who leads anything in the Enterprise — an entity, a team,
a project, or a single deliverable.

1. **Clarity is kindness.** Communicate directly, concisely, and without
   filler. Ambiguity is a cost someone else pays.
2. **Think in decades, act in weeks.** Every plan must serve the hundred-year
   vision and produce visible motion this month.
3. **Evidence before conviction, conviction before action.** Form views from
   data and clinical-grade rigor; then commit fully and execute.
4. **Slow down at one-way doors; move fast through two-way doors.**
   Irreversible decisions get written analysis and escalation. Reversible
   decisions get speed and a bias to act. (Article V defines the classes.)
5. **Delegate outcomes, not tasks.** Push every decision to the lowest level
   with the competence and context to make it, give them the standard and the
   authority, and hold them accountable for the result — not the method.
6. **Automate the repeatable; humanize the exceptional.** Machines handle
   repetition and scale. Humans handle judgment, relationships, exceptions,
   and everything behind a firewall.
7. **Surface problems early; never fail silently.** Bad news travels fastest.
   Reporting a problem early is rewarded; hiding one is a breach of trust.
8. **Protect the firewalls.** Clinical, legal, and PHI matters never move
   below licensed, designated authority. No deadline, opportunity, or
   automation overrides a firewall (Article VI, §4).
9. **Never trade the brand for the quarter.** No short-term gain justifies
   spending trust, safety, or the standard. Reputation compounds; so does its
   loss.
10. **Challenge ideas hard, respect people always.** Disagree openly and in
    writing before the decision; commit completely after it. Dissent is
    recorded, never punished.

## Article V — Decision Framework

Every significant decision in the Enterprise runs through the same two tools:
**classification** (who decides, at what speed) and **the Five Gates** (what
any decision must pass).

### §1. Decision Classes

| Class | Nature | Examples | Who decides | Protocol |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Class 1 — Constitutional** | Touches identity, values, firewalls, or this document | Amending PEGS-000, changing mission of an entity, altering a firewall | Founder (later: Stewardship Council) | Amendment process, Article VIII |
| **Class 2 — Structural (one-way doors)** | Irreversible or very costly to reverse | Forming/acquiring/closing an entity, key executive hires, major capital commitments, licensing, anything with legal exposure | Founder, informed by a written decision memo | Memo states the decision, evidence, Five Gates result, risks, and the dissent of anyone who disagrees. No verbal-only Class 2 decisions. |
| **Class 3 — Operational (two-way doors)** | Reversible in the normal course of business | Campaigns, content, pricing tests, vendor choices, process changes, hiring below key-executive level | The accountable role holder, per their documented decision rights | Act, record, inform. Speed is the default; asking permission for a Class 3 decision inside your rights is itself a process failure. |

When the class is unclear, treat the decision as one class higher and escalate
once. The person who escalates a genuinely unclear decision is never wrong.

### §2. The Five Gates

A significant decision must pass all five. A single failed gate is a veto.

1. **Mission Gate** — Does this sustain the people we serve? Would we be proud
   to explain it to a patient, a reader, and a grandchild?
2. **Integrity Gate** — Is every claim true and evidenced? Is anyone being
   manipulated, misled, or put at risk?
3. **Standard Gate** — Does this meet the Enterprise standard of craft? Would
   we sign it?
4. **Leverage Gate** — Does this compound? Does it create a system, an asset,
   or a capability — or just consume effort once?
5. **Legacy Gate** — Will this still look like a good decision in ten years?
   Does it strengthen what the next steward inherits?

### §3. Disagreement Protocol

Before the decision: disagreement is a duty, delivered directly and in
writing. After the decision: full commitment is a duty, regardless of the
dissent. Written dissents are preserved with the decision memo — they are how
the Enterprise learns, and they protect the dissenter.

## Article VI — Governance Philosophy

### §1. The Written Canon
If it is not written, it is not policy. The Enterprise is governed by written,
versioned documents — the PEGS canon — each with exactly one authoritative
location and one accountable custodian. Memory, chat threads, and verbal
agreements are never authoritative. Conflicts between documents resolve upward:
the more constitutional document wins, and PEGS-000 wins over everything.

### §2. Ratification
No document becomes canon by drift. Documents enter force only by explicit
ratification by the authority for their class, and are amended the same way.
Session learnings, experiments, and improvisations are valuable — and are
nothing until ratified.

### §3. Subsidiarity with Accountability
Authority lives at the lowest competent level; accountability travels with it.
Every role in the Enterprise has documented decision rights (its Class 3
territory) and documented escalation duties (its Class 2 triggers). Centralize
standards; decentralize execution.

### §4. The Firewalls
Three firewalls are permanent features of the Enterprise. They are structural,
not situational — no schedule pressure, growth target, or technology waives
them:

1. **Clinical/Legal/PHI Firewall.** Clinical judgments, legal commitments, and
   protected health information are handled only by licensed and designated
   authority. These are never delegated to unlicensed staff, outsourced to
   convenience, or automated past human accountability.
2. **Brand Firewall.** The Founder's personal brand sustains and never sells;
   commercial brands sell and never impersonate the Founder's personal voice.
   Each brand speaks only through its own ratified channels and canon.
3. **Compliance Floor.** Applicable law and professional regulation (health
   privacy, advertising truthfulness, professional licensure, and their
   successors anywhere the Enterprise operates) are the floor beneath every
   other consideration. There is no business case against the floor.

### §5. Automation Doctrine
The Enterprise automates by default: repeatable work is documented, then
templated, then automated, in that order. But automation serves the mission —
every automated system has a named human owner, a way to inspect its work, and
a hard boundary at the firewalls. We automate the repeatable and reserve human
judgment for the exceptional, the relational, and the firewalled.

### §6. Stewardship and Succession
Every critical role — starting with the Founder's — maintains a documented
succession path. Family members are welcomed into the Enterprise and are held
to this Constitution exactly as any executive: roles are earned by competence
and character, held in trust, and surrendered when a better steward exists.
The test of every leader is the strength of the Enterprise after they leave.

### §7. Constitutional Review
Once a year, the Enterprise's leadership reads this document together,
reaffirms it or proposes amendments through Article VIII, and records that the
review occurred. A constitution that is not read governs nothing.

## Article VII — Founder Philosophy

The Enterprise carries the Founder's operating philosophy, recorded here so it
can outlive him.

**No curo. Sostengo.** *I do not cure. I sustain.* Medicine taught the
Founder that the deepest service is not the dramatic rescue but the steady
sustaining of a human being over time. The Enterprise applies that lesson to
everything: patients, readers, employees, brands, and the family itself. We
are not in the rescue business. We are in the sustaining business.

**"To be what you want, there is only one obstacle: yourself."** Ownership of
outcomes begins with ownership of self. The Enterprise does not blame markets,
staff, or circumstances; it asks first what we must change in ourselves — our
systems, our clarity, our discipline.

**Physician first.** The Enterprise was born in the exam room. Whatever it
becomes, it retains the physician's ethic: first do no harm, evidence over
anecdote, the person in front of you over the abstraction, and the humility to
say "I don't know."

**Build the bridge.** The Founder built his life between two languages and two
cultures. The Enterprise exists partly to be that bridge at scale — to give
communities the same excellence in Spanish and English, and to prove the two
never required a trade-off.

**Teach what you master.** Knowledge hoarded dies with its holder. Every
mastered skill in the Enterprise becomes a book, a system, a training, or a
successor. The Founder's ambition is not to be irreplaceable; it is to be
well-replaced.

**Presence over performance.** The Enterprise values the real over the
performed: real patients over vanity metrics, real craft over volume, real
rest over hustle theater. Sustainability includes the people doing the
sustaining.

## Article VIII — Supremacy, Immutability, and Amendment

### §1. Supremacy
This Constitution is the supreme document of the Enterprise. Every PEGS
document, entity charter, role definition, brand canon, contract template, and
operating system must reference PEGS-000 and must not conflict with it. Any
conflict, ambiguity, or silence resolves in favor of this document.

### §2. The Immutable Core (Tier 0)
The following may never be amended, suspended, or waived — by anyone,
including the Founder, and including by unanimous consent:

1. Human dignity and safety outrank every commercial consideration (Article III, §2).
2. Integrity before advantage: the Enterprise does not deceive (Article III, §3).
3. The essence of the mission: the Enterprise exists to sustain (Article I).
4. The existence of the firewalls (Article VI, §4). Their implementation may
   evolve; their existence may not.
5. Stewardship: the Enterprise is held in trust, never merely owned (Article III, §7).
6. This clause.

A proposal that touches the Immutable Core is not an amendment; it is a
proposal to found a different enterprise, and it fails automatically.

### §3. Amendment of Everything Else (Tier 1)
All other provisions may be amended by the following process, and only by it:

1. **Written proposal** stating the current text, proposed text, and the
   reason — including what changed in the world to justify the change.
2. **Reflection period** of no fewer than 30 days. No amendment is ratified in
   the same season of emotion that proposed it.
3. **Ratification** by the Founder (or, after succession, by the Stewardship
   Council as defined in the 100-series charters), recorded in writing.
4. **Versioning**: the version number increments, the amendment is logged in
   the PEGS registry, and the superseded text is preserved in history — the
   Enterprise never erases its record.

### §4. Precedence of This Article
If any other provision of this Constitution is read to conflict with Article
VIII, Article VIII prevails.
