# Heir Education — raising stewards, not heirs

Inheritance without formation is a liability transfer. This path develops
the next generation into people who could be *chosen* for stewardship on
merit (PEGS-000 Art. III §7 — earned, never inherited).

## Stages (age-appropriate, adapt per person)

| Stage | Focus | Practices |
|---|---|---|
| Foundations (child/teen) | Values lived at home; both languages; work ethic | Age-appropriate jobs; money basics (earn/save/give/spend); service together |
| Literacy (16–22) | How the Enterprise actually works | Reads the Enterprise Charter, then PEGS-000 with the Founder; summer work INSIDE an entity, entry-level, reviewed like anyone |
| Apprenticeship (20s) | Competence somewhere ELSE first | 3–5 years outside employment — promotions earned where the surname is worthless; then optional entry via the standard hiring process |
| Stewardship candidacy (30+) | Governance formation | Observer seat at council meetings; heir completes a real project with P&L or mission accountability; annual read of the full canon |

## The conversations (calendared, not left to chance)

Annual "state of the family wealth" talk (age-appropriate transparency) ·
the succession roadmap walked through yearly · each heir's own ambitions
honored — the Enterprise serves the family's people, not the reverse; an
heir who chooses another path remains fully family.

## Disqualifiers (said out loud, early)

Entitlement behavior, integrity breaches, refusal of the path → no
stewardship role, regardless of surname. The family constitution makes
this a promise to the Enterprise's people, not a threat to heirs.
