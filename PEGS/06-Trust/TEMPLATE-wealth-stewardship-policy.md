# Wealth Stewardship Policy — <family/trust name>

> What the wealth is for, and the rules that keep it serving that purpose
> across generations. Governance substance; counsel embeds it in the legal
> trust instruments.

## 1. Purpose of the wealth (rank-ordered)

1. Security floor for every family member (health, housing, education)
2. Formation of stewards (education path fully funded, always)
3. The Enterprise's endurance (patient capital, never forced sales)
4. Philanthropy (via `05-Foundation` governance)
5. Opportunity capital for family ventures (earned access, below)

## 2. Distribution philosophy

- Needs before wants; formation before comfort; never idleness-funding.
- Age/stage gates over lump sums; trustee discretion guided by this policy.
- Transparency: adult members see the annual stewardship report.

## 3. Family venture capital (opportunity, not entitlement)

| Rule | Term |
|---|---|
| Access | Written plan + Five Gates review + outside-work track record |
| Structure | Investment or loan on recorded terms — never silent gifts |
| Limit | ≤ <X>% of liquid assets per person per decade |
| Failure | Honored as tuition if honest; concealment ends access |

## 4. Guardrails

No leverage against Enterprise control assets · no co-signing outside
policy · concentration limits · spending floor/ceiling bands reviewed
annually · every exception is a written, recorded decision.

## 5. Roles

Trustee(s) · investment advisor · the family council's voice · annual
stewardship report at a family meeting (plain language, both languages).
